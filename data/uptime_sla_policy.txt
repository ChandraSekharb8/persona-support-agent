Uptime and SLA Policy Guide

Overview
The platform is designed to provide reliable service availability for customers. Uptime, incident response, and service credits depend on the customer plan and contract terms.

Service Availability
The platform monitors:
- Application uptime
- API availability
- Dashboard performance
- Database connectivity
- Background job processing
- Third-party integration health

Incident Severity Levels

Severity 1 - Critical
A complete service outage or major production failure affecting many customers.

Expected response:
- Immediate investigation
- Status page update
- Engineering escalation
- Frequent customer updates

Severity 2 - High
Important features are degraded but the platform is partially usable.

Expected response:
- Priority investigation
- Workaround if available
- Customer communication
- Monitoring until resolved

Severity 3 - Medium
Minor feature issue affecting limited users.

Expected response:
- Standard support review
- Troubleshooting steps
- Fix scheduled based on priority

Severity 4 - Low
General questions, cosmetic bugs, or documentation clarification.

Expected response:
- Normal support response
- No urgent engineering escalation needed

Business Impact Communication
For business executives, support responses should focus on:
- Operational impact
- Estimated resolution guidance
- Current workaround
- Next update timeline
- Risk level

Status Page
Customers can check platform availability from the public status page. The status page may include:
- Current incidents
- Scheduled maintenance
- Past incident history
- Service component health

Scheduled Maintenance
Maintenance windows are announced in advance when possible. During scheduled maintenance, some features may be temporarily unavailable.

Service Credits
Service credit eligibility depends on contract terms and confirmed outage duration. Automated support should not promise service credits.

Escalation Conditions
Escalate to human support if:
- Customer reports production outage
- Executive asks for business impact or SLA credit
- Service is unavailable for multiple users
- Legal or contractual SLA concern is raised
- Customer asks for official incident report
