Billing Policy Guide

Overview
Our SaaS platform billing system manages subscriptions, invoices, renewals, taxes, plan upgrades, and payment history. Billing details are available from the Billing section inside Account Settings.

Billing Dashboard Location
Users can view billing information from:

Account Settings -> Billing -> Subscription & Invoices

The Billing page shows:

* Current subscription plan
* Renewal date
* Invoice history
* Payment method
* Tax details
* Billing contact email

Invoice Generation
Invoices are generated automatically after every successful subscription payment. Customers can download invoices from the Billing dashboard.

Steps to download an invoice:

1. Go to Account Settings.
2. Open Billing.
3. Select Invoice History.
4. Choose the required invoice month.
5. Click Download Invoice.

Payment Failure
A payment may fail because of:

* Expired card
* Insufficient balance
* Bank decline
* Incorrect billing address
* International payment restriction
* Payment gateway timeout

Resolution steps:

1. Ask the customer to verify the payment method.
2. Ask the customer to update card details.
3. Ask the customer to retry payment after 10 minutes.
4. If payment fails again, suggest using another payment method.
5. If the bank declined the payment, ask the customer to contact their bank.

Subscription Renewal
Subscriptions renew automatically at the end of the billing cycle if auto-renewal is enabled. Customers receive renewal reminders before the billing date.

Plan Upgrade
When a customer upgrades their plan, the new plan becomes active immediately. The customer may be charged a prorated amount based on the remaining days in the current billing cycle.

Plan Downgrade
When a customer downgrades their plan, the change is applied from the next billing cycle. Existing premium features may remain available until the end of the current cycle.

Duplicate Charges
Duplicate charges are rare but can happen when:

* A payment retry succeeds after an initial timeout
* The bank temporarily shows an authorization hold
* The customer clicks pay multiple times
* The payment gateway sends a delayed confirmation

Resolution steps:

1. Ask the customer to check whether both charges are fully settled or one is pending.
2. Ask the customer to wait 24–48 hours for pending holds to disappear.
3. If both charges are settled, escalate to billing support.
4. Include invoice number, payment date, amount, and customer account email in the escalation.

Refunds
Refund requests must be reviewed by the billing team. Automated support should not promise refund approval.

Escalation Conditions
Escalate to human support if:

* The customer demands a refund
* Duplicate charges are confirmed
* Legal or tax-related billing concerns are raised
* The customer reports unauthorized payment
* Enterprise invoice corrections are required
* Account ownership or billing contact change is requested
